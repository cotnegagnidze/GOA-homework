Mario's History

Mario first jumped into the world of gaming in 1981, making his debut in the arcade classic Donkey Kong. Originally referred to as "Jumpman," Mario quickly became a fan favorite due to his daring efforts to rescue Pauline.

In 1985, Mario starred in Super Mario Bros., revolutionizing video games with its side-scrolling platform style. The game introduced iconic elements, such as power-ups, pipes, and the Mushroom Kingdom.

Mario’s journey continued through various games, including Super Mario 64, Super Mario Galaxy, and Super Mario Odyssey. Each game has showcased Mario’s adventures in new worlds, solidifying his status as a global gaming legend.

1981: Donkey Kong

Mario's first appearance as a courageous carpenter rescuing Pauline.

1985: Super Mario Bros.

The game that revolutionized platform gaming and introduced the Mushroom Kingdom.

2007: Super Mario Galaxy

A space-themed adventure that redefined 3D gaming with gravity-defying levels.
